1. Introduction

Crime rates are increasing at an exponential rate day by day. Gun violence is one of the major crimes in the united states of America. Analyzing the gun violence data would help us to know how many incidents happen over a period of time,how many people are affected by the incident,what age group are likely to participate in these incidents etc. Analyzing this data may help in knowing what part of the country has highest gun violence.

The project involves working on a Gun violence dataset to analyse the outcomes of different machine learning algorithms based on few of the hypothesis assumed. Few of the major tasks involved were :
- Cleaning and preprocessing the data which involved taking care of the missing values
- breaking down the columns so that the features chosen were relevant to the hypothesis
- applying machine learning algorithms to solve the relevant hypothesis

3. Data set

The dataset we have chosen is Gun violence data with records dated from 2013 to 2018. The source of the dataset is Kaggle. The entire data set has 260k records and 29 features. The data set is of 34MB size. Out of the 29 features, we will only be using certain features for our analysis. In order to obtain the required data we will have to do preprocessing and combine data from multiple features. The following features are the once which we will be using :
- State (indicates state of the incident): used to classify the region of crime (West, Midwest, Northeast, Southwest, Southeast, Pacific)
- Participant_age_group, participant_type: used to classify the age group of the suspect
- Participant_type, participant_status: used to classify status of suspect after the incident
- Data: used to classify gun violence based on year
- n_killed , n_injured: Used to classify the severity of the incidence
- Participant_gender, Participant_type: used to classify the gender of the suspect.

4. Algorithms Implemented

Logistic Regression & KNN Algorithms have been implemented in Hadoop Map reduce to classify various hypothesis, where as Naive Bayes has been implemented on Spark.

Naive Bayes

We used this classification algorithm to solve hypothesis like In which coast of United states is the gun incident likely to occur? We are dividing the nation into 6 coasts. West, Midwest, Northeast, Southwest, Southeast, Pacific What age group do the participants usually belong to? We are classifying this into Adult and Teen, and Child

Logistic Regression

For binary classification problem where we had to solve the hypothesis Which gender group is most likely to be participating in the gun incident. Here we are considering whether the suspect is male or female.

K-Nearest Neighbors

We used KNN to solve the hypothesis : What is the severity of the incident. We shall classify this based on the number of killed and injured participants. We shall bin it into Low, Medium, and High.
